# LEVEL 0 CHARACTERS
---

Level-0 characters represent ordinary people before becoming true adventurers.

They are fragile and poorly equipped.

If they survive early adventures they may advance into full ICRPG classes.

## Ability Modifiers

Roll 3d6 for each stat.

## Hearts

Level-0 characters roll for starting HP.

HP = 1d4 + 2 + CON modifier

Minimum HP cannot be lower than 1.

This represents ordinary villagers with widely varying toughness.

When a Level-0 character earns their first Milestone, they become a Level-1 character and gain 1 full Heart (10 HP).
Their Level-0 HP does not carry forward.

# HERO COINS
---

Each PC begins every session with 1 Hero Coin.

PCs cannot gain additional personal Hero Coins.

The party begins with 1 shared Hero Coin.

The party may gain one additional Hero Coin during play.

Maximum party pool: 2

## Party Hero Coin Gain

### Party Coin Gain Conditions

# EQUIPMENT AND LOAD
---

Items are divided into Equipped Items and Packed Items.

Load capacity is determined by Strength.

## Carry Capacity

### Load Capacity

10 + STR modifier

## Equipped Items

Equipped items are immediately usable.

Examples include:

weapons
shields
belt gear
worn armor
held light sources

### Equipped Item Limit

5 + STR modifier

## Packed Items

Packed items are stored in containers such as backpacks or sacks.

Accessing packed items requires time during exploration or combat.

## Packs and Sacks

Containers determine how much Packed gear a character can carry.

### No Container

A character without a container can only carry Equipped Items.

Maximum carried items = Equipped Item Limit.

### Backpack

A backpack allows a character to carry Packed Items.

Maximum carried items = Load Capacity.

### Sack

A sack allows additional overflow beyond normal load.

Maximum carried items = Load Capacity + 4 slots.


## Item Load

### Item Load Table

Light item → 1 slot
Standard item → 2 slots
Heavy item → 3 slots
Very heavy item → 4+ slots

# SCOUTING
---

A character may scout from elevated terrain.

Roll INT or WIS.

## Success Procedure

1 Determine terrain visibility using the Terrain Visibility Table.

2 Reveal terrain type of all hexes within that range.

3 Reveal visible roads or rivers within that range.

4 Perform a Landmark Check for the farthest visible hex.


## Failure Procedure

### Scouting Failure Table

1–2 No useful information discovered
3–4 Waste time — lose one travel segment
5 False lead — incorrect terrain interpretation
6 Noise attracts attention — perform encounter check
7 Scout exposed — disadvantage on next encounter roll
8 Misread tracks — false danger sign
9 Lose direction briefly — navigation check required
10 Dangerous terrain discovered too late
11 Minor accident while scouting
12 Immediate encounter
